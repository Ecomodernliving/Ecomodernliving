# Passive House FAQ

> 101 original FAQs for EcoModernLiving.ai — derived from CPHD training concepts, rewritten for web and AI use.

## Definitions

### What is Passive House?

A building performance standard where year-round thermal comfort is achieved primarily by conditioning the fresh air required for healthy indoor air quality, without relying on large conventional heating or cooling systems.

**Related:** [/passive-house](/passive-house) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles) · [/guides/passive-house](/guides/passive-house)

### What is EnerPHit?

The Passive House Institute retrofit standard for existing buildings, with slightly relaxed airtightness (≤1.0 ACH50) and climate-specific heating demand limits compared to new construction.

**Related:** [/passive-house](/passive-house) · [/guides/passive-house](/guides/passive-house)

### What is PHPP?

Passive House Planning Package — the energy modeling software used to verify heating/cooling demand, loads, primary energy, and certification criteria before construction.

**Related:** [/passive-house/design](/passive-house/design) · [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators)

### What is TFA (Treated Floor Area)?

Net usable floor area in Passive House modeling, excluding internal walls and certain stair volumes, used as the reference area for energy metrics.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators)

### What is ACH50 / n50?

Air changes per hour at 50 Pascals pressure difference during a blower door test. New Passive House buildings must achieve ≤0.6 ACH50.

**Related:** [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles) · [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators)

### What is U-Factor?

Thermal transmittance measuring how quickly heat flows through a building component. Lower U-values mean better insulation. Windows use U-factor; opaque assemblies often use R-value.

**Related:** [/marketplace/windows](/marketplace/windows) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### What is R-Value?

Thermal resistance of insulation or assemblies (hr·ft²·°F/Btu). Higher R-values reduce heat loss through walls, roofs, and floors.

**Related:** [/marketplace/insulation](/marketplace/insulation) · [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators)

### What is Psi-value (Ψ)?

Linear thermal bridge coefficient (Btu/hr·ft·°F) describing heat loss along junctions such as wall-to-floor connections.

**Related:** [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles) · [/passive-house/envelope](/passive-house/envelope)

### What is Chi-value (χ)?

Point thermal bridge coefficient (Btu/hr·°F) for discrete penetrations like structural ties through insulation.

**Related:** [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### What is SHGC (g-value)?

Solar Heat Gain Coefficient — the fraction of solar radiation that passes through glazing as heat. Critical for balancing winter gains and summer overheating.

**Related:** [/marketplace/windows](/marketplace/windows) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### What is HRV / ERV?

Heat Recovery Ventilator or Energy Recovery Ventilator — balanced ventilation systems that recover heat (and sometimes moisture) from exhaust air to precondition incoming fresh air.

**Related:** [/marketplace/passive-house-materials](/marketplace/passive-house-materials) · [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac)

### What is PER (Primary Energy Renewable)?

Primary energy accounting that reflects renewable energy supply factors, used in Passive House Plus and Premium certification classes.

**Related:** [/passive-house](/passive-house) · [/guides/incentives](/guides/incentives)

### What is Operative Temperature?

Combined measure of air temperature and radiant surface temperatures that determines perceived thermal comfort per ISO 7730.

**Related:** [/guides/healthy-homes](/guides/healthy-homes) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### What is Thermal Bridge?

A localized area where insulation is interrupted or thinned, creating a path for heat to bypass the thermal envelope.

**Related:** [/passive-house/envelope](/passive-house/envelope) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### What is SAR (Surface Area Ratio)?

Total exterior envelope area divided by treated floor area. Higher SAR means more heat-losing surface per unit of living space.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators) · [/passive-house/design](/passive-house/design)

## Fundamentals

### Can you open windows in a Passive House?

Yes. Passive House buildings are not sealed boxes. Windows open normally; mechanical ventilation provides consistent fresh air when closed.

**Related:** [/passive-house-faq](/passive-house-faq)

### Is Passive House only for single-family homes?

No. The standard applies to offices, schools, multifamily housing, retrofits, and mixed-use buildings worldwide.

**Related:** [/passive-house](/passive-house)

### What are the five core Passive House principles?

Continuous insulation, airtight construction, high-performance windows and doors, thermal-bridge-free design, and balanced ventilation with heat recovery.

**Related:** [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### How is Passive House different from net zero?

Passive House limits energy demand through the building envelope first. Net zero focuses on offsetting annual energy use, often with renewables. Many Passive House projects also achieve net zero.

**Related:** [/guides/net-zero](/guides/net-zero)

### What is the Passive House comfort definition?

Thermal comfort per ISO 7730 should be achievable by post-heating or post-cooling the ventilation air stream alone, without recirculating large volumes of conditioned air.

**Related:** [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### Does Passive House ban active heating and cooling?

No. Active systems are sized small and used as backup. The goal is to minimize — not eliminate — mechanical heating and cooling.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac)

### What internal temperature is used in PHPP?

68°F with 50% relative humidity is the standard interior design condition for residential Passive House modeling.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators)

### Why use exterior dimensions for heat loss?

Passive House uses exterior envelope dimensions for a conservative, reproducible calculation of transmission losses.

**Related:** [/passive-house/envelope](/passive-house/envelope)

### What is the Saskatchewan Conservation House?

A 1977 Canadian prototype that demonstrated super insulation, triple glazing, heat-recovery ventilation, and early airtightness testing — a direct ancestor of the Passive House standard.

**Related:** [/passive-house](/passive-house) · [/guides/passive-house](/guides/passive-house)

### What software is used for Passive House energy modeling?

The Passive House Planning Package (PHPP) is the primary certification tool. designPH provides a SketchUp-based interface for PHPP workflows.

**Related:** [/passive-house/design](/passive-house/design) · [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators)

### Why is design the envelope first important in Passive House design?

Prioritize insulation, airtightness, and window performance before sizing mechanical systems.

**Related:** [/passive-house](/passive-house) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles) · [/guides/passive-house](/guides/passive-house)

### Why is model in phpp early important in Passive House design?

Use energy modeling during schematic design to test form factor, orientation, and insulation thickness.

**Related:** [/passive-house](/passive-house) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles) · [/guides/passive-house](/guides/passive-house)

### Why is compact building form important in Passive House design?

Simpler, more compact massing reduces envelope area and lowers insulation requirements.

**Related:** [/passive-house](/passive-house) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles) · [/guides/passive-house](/guides/passive-house)

## Building Envelope

### What R-values do Passive House walls typically need?

Climate and form factor dependent. Cool-temperate single-family homes often need R-38 to R-60+ opaque assemblies; multifamily compact forms may need less.

**Related:** [/marketplace/insulation](/marketplace/insulation)

### What is a good A/V ratio?

Lower is better. Multistory apartments can reach 0.08–0.10; single-story homes may exceed 0.30 and require more insulation.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators)

### Is more insulation always better?

No. Each additional inch yields diminishing returns. PHPP optimization balances cost and performance.

**Related:** [/passive-house/costs](/passive-house/costs)

### Can spray foam alone achieve Passive House airtightness?

Spray foam insulates well but is not a dedicated air barrier product. Dedicated membranes, tapes, and detailing are still required for ≤0.6 ACH50.

**Related:** [/passive-house/envelope](/passive-house/envelope)

### Does exterior insulation eliminate thermal bridging?

Exterior insulation greatly reduces bridging but fasteners, balconies, and window penetrations can still create bridges if not detailed carefully.

**Related:** [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### What interior conditions risk condensation?

At 68°F and 50% RH, surface temperatures below ~54°F increase mold risk; below ~49°F condensation risk rises sharply on cold surfaces.

**Related:** [/guides/healthy-homes](/guides/healthy-homes)

### Why is continuous insulation important in Passive House design?

Wrap the thermal envelope without gaps so heat cannot bypass insulation layers.

**Related:** [/passive-house/envelope](/passive-house/envelope) · [/marketplace/insulation](/marketplace/insulation)

### Why is control moisture drive important in Passive House design?

Place vapor control layers on the warm side in heating climates and on the exterior in hot-humid climates.

**Related:** [/passive-house/envelope](/passive-house/envelope) · [/marketplace/insulation](/marketplace/insulation)

### Why is right-size insulation important in Passive House design?

Add insulation until diminishing returns; PHPP finds the economic and performance sweet spot.

**Related:** [/passive-house/envelope](/passive-house/envelope) · [/marketplace/insulation](/marketplace/insulation)

## Airtightness

### Why is airtightness critical?

It prevents drafts, moisture damage in assemblies, uncontrolled heat loss, noise infiltration, and undermines ventilation system performance.

**Related:** [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### What materials work for long-term airtightness?

Liquid-applied membranes, specialized tapes, and well-cast concrete (when crack-free) — not standard DIY caulk or duct tape.

**Related:** [/marketplace/passive-house-materials](/marketplace/passive-house-materials)

### When should blower door testing happen?

At a point when the air barrier is accessible — typically after windows are installed and before drywall — and again at final completion.

**Related:** [/passive-house/envelope](/passive-house/envelope)

### How is Vn50 calculated?

Treated floor area multiplied by 8.2 ft standard ceiling height defines the reference test volume for most residential projects.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators)

### What is reported from blower door tests?

The average of pressurization and depressurization results at 50 Pa, expressed as n50 (ACH).

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators)

### Do large buildings have extra airtightness requirements?

Buildings over 53,000 ft³ must meet n50 ≤0.6 and should report q50; target q50 <0.034 CFM/ft² is recommended.

**Related:** [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### Can ventilation ducts be sealed during testing?

Yes — exterior intake and exhaust openings should be sealed during the test to avoid false leakage through ductwork.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac)

### Why is define the air barrier early important in Passive House design?

Identify the continuous airtight layer on drawings before construction begins.

**Related:** [/passive-house/envelope](/passive-house/envelope) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### Why is test progressively important in Passive House design?

Conduct blower door tests at rough-in and again at completion to catch leaks when they are accessible.

**Related:** [/passive-house/envelope](/passive-house/envelope) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### Why is prioritize air transport over diffusion important in Passive House design?

A 1-inch air leak moves far more moisture than vapor diffusion through intact materials.

**Related:** [/passive-house/envelope](/passive-house/envelope) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

## Windows & Doors

### What window U-value do Passive House projects target?

Installed U-values often need to be ≤0.14–0.17 Btu/(hr·ft²·°F) in cold climates, depending on climate and orientation mix.

**Related:** [/marketplace/windows](/marketplace/windows)

### What is triple-pane low-e glazing?

Three glass panes with low-emissivity coatings and argon or krypton gas fill, reducing conduction and radiation heat transfer.

**Related:** [/marketplace/windows](/marketplace/windows)

### What is the window energy criterion Ug·g?

A simplified metric where lower U and appropriate SHGC together indicate climate-suitable glazing performance.

**Related:** [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### Why do window frames matter?

Frames conduct heat differently than glass. Uf, spacer psi-values, and frame-to-glass ratio all affect comfort and energy.

**Related:** [/marketplace/windows](/marketplace/windows)

### Should shading be interior or exterior?

Exterior shading blocks solar gain before it enters the building and is far more effective for summer comfort.

**Related:** [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### Why is install with thermal bridge-free details important in Passive House design?

Use certified connection details and proper interior/exterior air seals at rough openings.

**Related:** [/marketplace/windows](/marketplace/windows) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### Why is balance u-value and shgc important in Passive House design?

Cold climates favor lower U and moderate-to-high SHGC on south facades; cooling climates need stronger shading.

**Related:** [/marketplace/windows](/marketplace/windows) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### Why is specify two-decimal shgc important in Passive House design?

Small differences in g-value significantly affect annual energy balance in PHPP.

**Related:** [/marketplace/windows](/marketplace/windows) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

## Ventilation

### Why can't you rely on window ventilation in Passive House?

Natural ventilation is unreliable, causes excessive heat loss in cold weather, and cannot guarantee consistent indoor air quality.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac)

### What airflow rate is typical?

Roughly 0.3–0.4 air changes per hour based on occupant count and humidity control needs, verified in PHPP.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac)

### HRV vs ERV — which to choose?

HRVs recover heat; ERVs also transfer moisture. Cold dry climates often use HRV; humid or mixed climates may benefit from ERV.

**Related:** [/marketplace/passive-house-materials](/marketplace/passive-house-materials)

### Can you heat a Passive House with ventilation air alone?

Often yes in mild climates via supply-air post-heating coils. Disconnected or extract-only rooms may need supplemental heat.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac)

### Why insulate ventilation ducts?

Prevent condensation and heat loss when warm exhaust ducts pass through cold zones or vice versa.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac)

### Why is use balanced hrv/erv important in Passive House design?

Supply and extract airflows should be balanced with ≥75% heat recovery for certification.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac) · [/marketplace/passive-house-materials](/marketplace/passive-house-materials)

### Why is zone rooms correctly important in Passive House design?

Bedrooms and living areas get supply air; kitchens and baths extract; transfer paths connect zones.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac) · [/marketplace/passive-house-materials](/marketplace/passive-house-materials)

### Why is filter fresh air to f7+ important in Passive House design?

High-quality filters protect occupants and keep heat exchangers clean.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac) · [/marketplace/passive-house-materials](/marketplace/passive-house-materials)

## Heating & Cooling

### What heating systems suit Passive House?

Mini-split heat pumps, ventilation-integrated coils, small hydronic panels, and electric resistance backup sized to low peak loads.

**Related:** [/marketplace/heat-pumps](/marketplace/heat-pumps)

### What is the overheating criterion?

Without active cooling, indoor temperatures should not exceed 77°F for more than 10% of annual hours.

**Related:** [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### How do you reduce cooling demand?

External shading, low SHGC glazing, night ventilation, airtight envelope, insulation, and minimizing internal gains.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac)

### Are radiators under windows still needed?

Rarely in Passive House. High-performance glazing eliminates cold downdrafts that conventional systems compensated for.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac)

### How does DHW compare to space heating load?

In Passive House, domestic hot water energy can equal or exceed space heating — efficient DHW design matters.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac)

### Why is right-size heat pumps important in Passive House design?

Size to peak load (≤3.17 Btu/hr·ft²), not conventional oversizing rules.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac) · [/marketplace/heat-pumps](/marketplace/heat-pumps)

### Why is leverage internal gains important in Passive House design?

People, appliances, and lighting offset heating demand — account for them in PHPP.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac) · [/marketplace/heat-pumps](/marketplace/heat-pumps)

### Why is shade before you cool important in Passive House design?

Reduce solar gains and internal loads before adding mechanical cooling capacity.

**Related:** [/passive-house/hvac](/passive-house/hvac) · [/marketplace/heat-pumps](/marketplace/heat-pumps)

## Economics & ROI

### Is Passive House more expensive to build?

Upfront costs can run 5–15% higher initially, but energy savings, comfort, durability, and incentives improve lifecycle economics.

**Related:** [/passive-house/costs](/passive-house/costs)

### What is cost of saved kBtu?

A metric comparing incremental investment to annual energy saved — useful for comparing envelope upgrades.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators)

### Do Passive House buildings have higher resale value?

Growing evidence shows lower operating costs, comfort, and certification status attract buyers and reduce vacancy.

**Related:** [/passive-house/costs](/passive-house/costs)

### What incentives apply in the US?

30% federal solar ITC, IRA heat pump rebates up to $8,000, EV credits, and state-specific efficiency programs.

**Related:** [/guides/incentives](/guides/incentives)

### Why is calculate lifecycle cost important in Passive House design?

Use annuity or present-value methods comparing extra envelope investment to decades of energy savings.

**Related:** [/passive-house/costs](/passive-house/costs) · [/guides/incentives](/guides/incentives)

### Why is stack incentives important in Passive House design?

Combine federal tax credits, IRA rebates, and state programs to offset envelope and heat pump costs.

**Related:** [/passive-house/costs](/passive-house/costs) · [/guides/incentives](/guides/incentives)

### Why is factor experience curve important in Passive House design?

First Passive House projects cost more; teams with repeat experience reduce premiums significantly.

**Related:** [/passive-house/costs](/passive-house/costs) · [/guides/incentives](/guides/incentives)

## Renewable Energy

### What is Passive House Plus?

Certification class requiring on-site or nearby renewable generation offsetting higher PER demand thresholds.

**Related:** [/passive-house](/passive-house)

### What is Passive House Premium?

The highest class — lowest PER demand and highest renewable generation requirements.

**Related:** [/passive-house](/passive-house)

### Should you add solar before insulating?

No. Envelope improvements deliver better ROI and reduce required PV capacity.

**Related:** [/guides/net-zero](/guides/net-zero)

### Why is efficiency before pv important in Passive House design?

Minimize demand with the envelope so solar and batteries can cover a larger share of needs.

**Related:** [/passive-house/solar](/passive-house/solar) · [/marketplace/solar](/marketplace/solar)

### Why is match generation to load important in Passive House design?

Size PV and storage based on PHPP primary energy and occupant usage patterns.

**Related:** [/passive-house/solar](/passive-house/solar) · [/marketplace/solar](/marketplace/solar)

## Calculations

### How do you calculate Transmission heat loss (annual)?

Annual heat loss through opaque or glazed envelope areas. Formula: QT = A × U × ft × Gt. Units: kBtu/yr. A = area (ft²), U = 1/R, ft = ground correction factor, Gt = heating degree hours.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### How do you calculate Transmission heat load (peak)?

Peak-hour heat loss used to size heating systems. Formula: PT = A × U × ft × ΔT. Units: Btu/hr.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### How do you calculate R-value (layered assembly)?

Sum interior film resistance, layer resistances, and exterior film resistance. Formula: R = Rsi + Σ(d × r) + Rse. Units: hr·ft²·°F/Btu.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### How do you calculate Linear thermal bridge loss?

Annual loss along linear junctions like slab edges. Formula: QT-tb = L × Ψ × ft × Gt. Units: kBtu/yr.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### How do you calculate Infiltration ventilation loss?

Heat loss from air leakage at resting pressure; e ≈ 0.07 commonly used. Formula: QV-infill = Vn50 × e × n50 × Cair × Gt. Units: kBtu/yr.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### How do you calculate n50 from blower door?

Convert CFM at 50 Pa to air changes per hour. Formula: n50 = (V50 × 60) / Vn50. Units: 1/h.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### How do you calculate q50 (large buildings)?

Surface-normalized leakage for buildings >53,000 ft³; target <0.034 CFM/ft² recommended. Formula: q50 = (V50 × 60) / EAn50. Units: CFM/ft².

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### How do you calculate Window installed U-value?

Weighted U-value including glass, frame, spacer, and installation losses. Formula: Uw = (Ug×Ag + Uf×Af + Ψspacer×Lspacer + Ψinstall×Linstall) / Awindow. Units: Btu/(hr·ft²·°F).

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### How do you calculate Window energy balance?

Net annual energy through glazing after solar gains and transmission losses. Formula: QS - QT = (r × SHGC × Aw × G) - (Aw × U × Gt). Units: kBtu/yr.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)

### How do you calculate Internal heat gains?

Residential qi typically 0.67–1.30 Btu/(hr·ft²). Formula: QI = 0.024 × Ht × qi × ATFA. Units: kBtu/yr.

**Related:** [/passive-house-calculators](/passive-house-calculators) · [/passive-house-principles](/passive-house-principles)
